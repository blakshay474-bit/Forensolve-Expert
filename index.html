Criminal Behavior

Modus Operandi Analysis

Understanding how criminals operate is key to prevention. We analyze and classify criminal methodologies across multiple dimensions.

MO Category 01

Premeditated & Planned Operations

Highly organized crimes involving extensive planning, surveillance, and preparation. Includes targeted assassinations, sophisticated heists, and calculated corporate fraud. Perpetrators often demonstrate high intelligence and counter-forensic awareness.

High Risk Low Detection Rate
MO Category 02

Opportunistic & Impulse Crimes

Crimes committed without prior planning, triggered by opportunity or emotional impulse. Includes crimes of passion, spontaneous theft, and road rage incidents. Often leaving more forensic evidence due to lack of preparation.

Medium Risk High Evidence Trail
MO Category 03

Serial & Repeat Offenders

Patterns of repeated criminal behavior with evolving sophistication. Includes serial killers, repeat sexual offenders, and habitual burglars. Behavioral analysis and geographic profiling are key tools for identification.

Critical Risk Pattern-Based
MO Category 04

Technology-Enabled Crimes

Criminal operations leveraging advanced technology — ransomware deployment, deepfake manipulation, AI-powered phishing, SIM swapping, cryptocurrency mixing laundering, and IoT device exploitation for surveillance or sabotage.

Rapidly Evolving High Complexity
MO Category 05

Organized Crime Syndicates

Hierarchically structured criminal networks engaged in drug trafficking, arms dealing, human trafficking, and extortion. These organizations employ counter-intelligence tactics, encrypted communications, and compartmentalized cell structures.

Multi-Jurisdictional Network Analysis
MO Category 06

Insider Threat & Espionage

Trusted individuals exploiting privileged access for sabotage, data theft, or espionage. Includes corporate insiders, government employees, and embedded agents. Behavioral analytics and access pattern monitoring are primary detection methods.

Hard to Detect Access-Based
